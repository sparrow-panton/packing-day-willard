Packing Day

It's April 1870, and you are Sarah Holloway, an artist and musician living in Ovid, New York. This morning a letter arrived from your doctor that changed everything.

Mrs. Sarah Holloway
Cedar Creek Farm
Ovid, New York

April 15, 1870

Dear Mrs. Holloway,

It is with careful consideration that we inform you of the decision reached by Dr. James Henderson, in consultation with your husband, regarding your recent episodes of nervous exhaustion and melancholia.

Your condition has been determined to be of a chronic nature requiring specialized treatment beyond what can be provided at home. Arrangements have been made for your admission to the Willard Asylum for the Chronic Insane on Thursday next.

The institution offers the most modern treatments for cases such as yours, in a peaceful setting by Seneca Lake. You will be under the care of competent physicians who specialize in diseases of the mind and nervous system.

You are permitted one small suitcase containing personal items. Please note that all items will be inspected upon arrival, and those deemed unsuitable or potentially harmful will be returned to your family.

A carriage will arrive at 9 o'clock Thursday morning to transport you to Willard.

Sincerely,

Edwin R. Sherman
Clerk of Seneca County

You are terrified. The Willard Asylum for the Chronic Insane? Is this really happening? You have heard stories whispered about that place. People go in, and never get out. You have until tomorrow morning to pack your things. What will you take with you?
